About Joycelyn Elder

Minnie Joycelyn Elders originally Minnie Lee Jones is a known American Pediatrician and Public Health Administrator. Elder is also the first African American as a Surgeon General of the United States. Jocelyn Elders was born August 13, 1933 in Schaal, Arkansas. Elders had experinece discrimination during her time of education but, that did not stop her from pursing her career because when she graduated highscholl she attended University of Arkansas for Medical Sciences and Philander Smith College. Being the only Black person in the Brooke Army Medical Center she started her intership as a physical therapist. Elder is best known for her controversial issues such as drug legalization, racial equality in medicine, and sex education. SHze had made a great impact on her family and community and shall be remenbered in African American History.
